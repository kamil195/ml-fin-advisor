# Freelancer Interview Guide — Fin Advisor Customer Validation

> **Goal:** Understand how freelancers/gig workers manage irregular income today,
> measure the intensity of their pain, and gauge reaction to our AI forecasting tool.
>
> **Duration:** 25–30 minutes
>
> **Golden rule:** Listen 80 %, talk 20 %. Never sell — just learn.

---

## Phase 0 · Before the Call (prep — 2 min)

| Check | Detail |
|---|---|
| Name / role / platform | Look them up so you can ask informed questions |
| Recording consent | Prepare to ask at the start |
| Landing page ready | Have `https://your-landing-page.com` open in a tab |
| Notes template open | Have `notes-template.md` ready to fill in live |

---

## Phase 1 · Introduction (2 min)

> *Read naturally — don't sound scripted.*

**Say something like:**

> "Hey [Name], thanks so much for taking the time. I really appreciate it.
>
> Quick context — I'm building a tool that helps people with variable income
> get a clearer picture of their finances. I'm *not* selling anything today.
> I'm genuinely trying to understand how freelancers manage money, so I can
> build something that actually helps.
>
> There are no right or wrong answers — I just want to hear your real
> experience. If something doesn't apply, just say so.
>
> Would it be okay if I take notes? [If recording:] And would you mind if I
> record this so I don't miss anything? It stays private — only for my notes."

**Transition:** *"Great. Let's start with a bit about you."*

---

## Phase 2 · Warm-Up — Their World (3 min)

The goal is to get them talking comfortably about their work before you touch
money topics.

| # | Question | What you're listening for |
|---|---|---|
| W1 | "Tell me a bit about what you do — what kind of freelance/gig work?" | Domain, platforms, tenure |
| W2 | "How long have you been freelancing full-time (or part-time)?" | Experience level |
| W3 | "What does a typical month look like income-wise — pretty steady, or does it bounce around?" | Income variability signal |

> **Probe if they say "it varies":**
> *"Can you give me a rough range? Like what's a good month vs a lean month?"*

---

## Phase 3 · Pain Exploration (8–10 min)

This is the heart of the interview. Go deep on emotions, not just facts.

### 3A — Current Money Management

| # | Question | Listen for |
|---|---|---|
| P1 | "Walk me through how you currently manage your money month to month." | Process, tools, habits |
| P2 | "Do you use any apps or tools to track income/expenses? Which ones?" | Competitors, satisfaction |
| P3 | "How do you decide what you can safely spend in a given month?" | Decision process — gut feel vs system |
| P4 | "Do you set budgets? If so, how — and do they actually hold up?" | Budget adherence, frustration |

### 3B — Pain & Frustration

| # | Question | Listen for |
|---|---|---|
| P5 | "What's the most stressful part about managing money with irregular income?" | **Core pain — write the exact words** |
| P6 | "Can you tell me about a specific time when income unpredictability caused a real problem?" | Story = strong signal |
| P7 | "Have you ever had a month where you spent too much early on because you expected income that didn't come?" | Over-spending pattern |
| P8 | "How much time per week do you spend thinking about or managing your finances?" | Time cost |
| P9 | "On a scale of 1–10, how confident do you feel about your financial situation in any given month?" | Confidence level |

> **Key probe after P5 or P6:**
> *"How did that make you feel? What did you end up doing?"*
>
> **If they mention a tool that failed them:**
> *"What was missing? What would have made it actually useful?"*

### 3C — Existing Solutions & Gaps

| # | Question | Listen for |
|---|---|---|
| P10 | "Have you tried any budgeting or forecasting tools? What happened?" | Churn reasons |
| P11 | "What would a perfect 'financial clarity' tool do for you?" | Wish list — their language |
| P12 | "Would you pay for something that reliably told you 'you can safely spend X this month'?" | WTP signal |

> **If they say yes to P12:**
> *"Roughly what would that be worth to you per month — $5, $10, $20?"*

---

## Phase 4 · Solution Presentation (5 min)

> **Only show the landing page AFTER the pain exploration is complete.**
> You want unbiased pain data first.

**Transition:**

> "Thanks — this is super helpful. I want to show you something we're
> working on and get your honest reaction. It's early stage, so brutal
> honesty helps me more than politeness."

**Share screen / send link to landing page.**

> *"Take a minute to read through it. I'll be quiet while you look."*

**Wait 30–60 seconds. Don't explain. Watch where they pause or react.**

### Reaction Capture Questions

| # | Question | Listen for |
|---|---|---|
| R1 | "What's your first impression?" | Gut reaction |
| R2 | "In your own words, what does this tool do?" | Comprehension — did the value prop land? |
| R3 | "Does this feel like it solves the problem you described earlier?" | Problem–solution fit |
| R4 | "What would make you skeptical about this actually working?" | Objections & trust barriers |
| R5 | "Would you sign up for an early access list right now?" | Commitment signal |
| R6 | "Who else do you know that would need this?" | Referral willingness = strong signal |

> **If they say "this looks interesting":**
> *"Interesting good, or interesting I'm-being-polite? Seriously — what would make you actually use it?"*

---

## Phase 5 · Closing (2 min)

> "This has been incredibly helpful — thank you.
>
> A couple of quick last things:"

| # | Question |
|---|---|
| C1 | "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?" |
| C2 | "Would you be open to trying a beta version when it's ready?" |
| C3 | "Do you know 1–2 other freelancers who might be willing to chat with me?" |

> "Thanks again, [Name]. I'll keep you posted on progress. Really appreciate your time."

---

## Interviewer Reminders

- **Silence is your friend.** After they answer, count to 3 before responding. They'll often add the most valuable detail in that pause.
- **Mirror their language.** If they say "feast or famine" — use that phrase back.
- **Write exact quotes.** Paraphrase loses the gold.
- **Don't pitch.** If you catch yourself explaining features, stop and ask a question instead.
- **Emotions > facts.** "How did that feel?" is more valuable than "How much was it?"
- **Follow the energy.** If they light up or get frustrated about something, go deeper there.
